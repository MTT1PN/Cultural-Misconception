Santa Cruz de la Sierra

Located in the department of Santa Cruz, south-east of Bolivia, its founded in the tropical lowlands near the Pirai river. Although the country is seen as Andean, only 30% of its territory consists of this culture. The vast majority is tropical like Santa Cruz. As a tropical department, most of his ecosystem consists of a lot of flora and exotic animals like the Jaguar and the Tucan. These are some landmarks that reflect this quality:

  • Jardin Botanico: The best way to explore nature without leaving the city. Extensive and well maintained grouds to explore and observe wild animals.
  • Biocentro Guembe: A cross between a water park and wildlife reserve. It counts with dozens of pools and a huge atrium.
  • Amboro National Park: One of the most impressive national park in the country. Perfect to explore (with a certified guide) thanks to its abundant flora and wild life.

And from its history, being part of the Spanish colony, gave birth to several towns where to explore beautiful structures. Like San Javier, place of the Jesuit Missions with colorful churches and national and international cultural music festivals that nowadays takes place. Finally, Santa Cruz has what everyone loves the most, a huge variety of Food and Music.
