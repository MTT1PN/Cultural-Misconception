Sucre

Administrative Capital of Bolivia, was founded in a fertile valley by the Cachimayo River at 2800 meters above sea level and its located in the department of Chuquisaca in the south of Bolivia. Because of this city gave birth to the country, many museums and historical centers are located here inluding the Casa de la Libertad (House of Liberty) where the Declaration of Independence is to be displayed.

Most buildings in the city present Jesuit architecture, specially churchs and temples which some have become museums aswell. And talking about history, Sucre also posses a Cretaceous Park with many dinosaur footprints discovered in 1994. But if you are not into a lot of history, and even if you do, you can enjoy some nice Food and Music.

  • Cal Orck'o:This huge limestone, discovered in a concrete mine, has revealed up to 5055 dinosaur footprints among which a world record of 581-meters trail made by a Theropod. Plus, it counts with real size replicas and fosiles of these dinosaurs.
  • Monasteries/Temples: Santa Clara, San Felipe Neri, La Recoleta, are some of these beautiful designed and historic builidings. Because of their Spanish colony architecture, the city was designated a UNESCO World Heritage site in 1991.
